      <p>
        I am <strong>Saba Tabatabaee</strong>, a Ph.D. student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the 
        <a href="https://umd.edu/">University of Maryland, College Park</a>. I conduct my research in the 
        <a href="https://scl.umd.edu/">Speech Communication Lab</a> under the supervision of 
        <a href="https://isr.umd.edu/clark/faculty/391/Carol-Espy-Wilson">Prof. Carol Espy-Wilson</a>.
      </p>

      <p>
        My research lies at the intersection of speech processing, signal processing, linguistics, and machine learning. 
        I have worked on projects ranging from robust speaker verification in noisy classroom environments 
        to advanced speech enhancement methods.
      </p>

      <p>
        Currently, I am developing a speech inversion system that leverages AI to estimate vocal tract variables, such as 
        lip and tongue movements and nasal airflow, directly from far-field speech recordings. 
        This work has applications in both speech technology and clinical assessment.
      </p>
